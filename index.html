Correlation: Personality Traits vs. Attack Vulnerability

Openness to Experience

Curiosity can lead to clicking on unknown links or exploring "too good to be true" offers, making them targets for baiting and phishing with creative lures.

Conscientiousness

Typically the most resilient trait. Conscientious individuals follow rules and are cautious. However, they can be exploited by spear-phishing that simulates authority.

Extraversion

Extraverts are sociable and may overshare information. They are vulnerable to vishing and attacks that exploit rapport building and social networks.

Agreeableness

The desire to help and innate trust make agreeable individuals prime targets. They are highly susceptible to phishing emails that appeal to help or urgency.

Neuroticism

Neuroticism is linked to strong emotional reactions. Attacks that induce fear, anxiety, or urgency (e.g., "Your account has been compromised!") are particularly effective.

Intervention Effectiveness by Trait

Mitigation strategies are not universal. Personalized training and behavioral "nudges" show varied effectiveness depending on the individual's personality profile.

Personalized Risk and Mitigation Framework

Big Five Trait Elevated Risk Common Attack Vector Key Mitigation Strategy
Openness Moderate Baiting (creative lures), Phishing (novelty) Digital skepticism training, source validation.
Conscientiousness Low Spear-phishing (abuse of authority) Double-verification policies for unusual requests.
Extraversion Moderate Vishing, Smishing (social exploitation) Awareness training on information sharing (oversharing).
Agreeableness High Phishing (emotional appeals), Vishing (requests for help) Phishing simulations focused on empathy triggers.
Neuroticism High Phishing (urgency, fear), Smishing (false alerts) Training on stress management and pause-to-verify steps.

Actionable Conclusion

Integrating behavioral science into cybersecurity strategy allows the transition from a reactive defense to proactive, personalized resilience. By understanding individual risk profiles, organizations can implement trainings, policies, and nudges that are fundamentally more effective, strengthening their security posture from the inside out.
